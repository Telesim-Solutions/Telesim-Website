The Mission

Telesim Solutions is committed to addressing the needs of under equipped healthcare workers in Africa and other low resource settings by providing affordable, sustainable, and high impact education and training.

About

About Us

An estimated 1 million health care workers in Africa lack access to affordable education, training, and mentorship. The current training modality for health care workers in surveillance and case management of highly infectious diseases involves classroom and onsite practical instruction. This modality is impractical for a highly infectious disease like COVID-19 that requires rapid training to frontline healthcare workers while maintaining appropriate social distancing. The current training modality is associated with two fundamental gaps/needs:
1) In-person training is difficult to deliver rapidly and at scale without exponentially increasing the costs in time and money. This represents a major weakness when facing a condition with an exponential increasing infectious trajectory in a resource-limited setting.
2) In-person training also has a high probability of compromised social distancing requirements with risk of infection.

The Problem

Problem

Healthcare workers across Africa need better access to education and training to fight infectious diseases like COVID-19 and to strengthen the overall healthcare response in their communities. Community Health Workers are a critical point of delivery for prevention and treatment protocols in communities across Africa, yet many lack resources to obtain ongoing education. As of 2013, the needs-based health worker shortage in Africa was 4.2 million and is estimated to reach 6.1 million by 2030. Additionally, the demand for health workers in Africa is projected to double by 2030.
